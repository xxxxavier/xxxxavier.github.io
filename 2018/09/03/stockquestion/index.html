Stock problem

9月 03, 2018

Stock problem is an important part of dynamic programming. In Leetcode, there are six different question about the stock.

  • Best Time to Buy and Sell Stock
  • Best Time to Buy and Sell Stock II
  • Best Time to Buy and Sell Stock III
  • Best Time to Buy and Sell Stock IV
  • Best Time to Buy and Sell Stock with Cooldown
  • Best Time to Buy and Sell Stock with Transaction Fee

Two questions are easy, two questions are hard, and the remaining two are medium.

##Best Time to Buy and Sell Stock (easy)
Question:

Say you have an array for which the ith element is the price of a given stock on day i.

If you were only permitted to complete at most one transaction (i.e., buy one and sell one share of the stock), design an algorithm to find the maximum profit.

Note that you cannot sell a stock before you buy one.

Example:

Input: [7,1,5,3,6,4]

Output: 5

Explanation: Buy on day 2 (price = 1) and sell on day 5 (price = 6), profit = 6-1 = 5.
Not 7-1 = 6, as selling price needs to be larger than buying price.

This question is pretty easy, what we need is a variable to save the minimum value and result.

##Best Time to Buy and Sell Stock II
Question:

Say you have an array for which the ith<\sup> element is the price of a given stock on day i.

Design an algorithm to find the maximum profit. You may complete as many transactions as you like (i.e., buy one and sell one share of the stock multiple times).

Note: You may not engage in multiple transactions at the same time (i.e., you must sell the stock before you buy again).

Solution:

Input: [7,1,5,3,6,4]

Output: 7

Explanation: Buy on day 2 (price = 1) and sell on day 3 (price = 5), profit = 5-1 = 4.
Then buy on day 4 (price = 3) and sell on day 5 (price = 6), profit = 6-3 = 3.

This question is similar with previous question.

##Best Time to Buy and Sell Stock III
Question:

Say you have an array for which the ith element is the price of a given stock on day i.

Design an algorithm to find the maximum profit. You may complete at most two transactions.

Note: You may not engage in multiple transactions at the same time (i.e., you must sell the stock before you buy again).

Example:

Input: [3,3,5,0,0,3,1,4]

Output: 6

Explanation: Buy on day 4 (price = 0) and sell on day 6 (price = 3), profit = 3-0 = 3.
Then buy on day 7 (price = 1) and sell on day 8 (price = 4), profit = 4-1 = 3.

In this problem, my idea is create 2D matrix dp to save previous result. In this matrix, d[i][j] means the maximum value form day i to day j. The dynamic programming equation is dp[i][j] = max(dp[left][right], dp[i+1][k]+dp[k+1][j-1]) in this equation, dp[i+1][k]+dp[k+1][j-1] means we will sell our stock in the interval from i+1 to k and k+1 to j-1.

The problem of this solution is that if the size of input prices is very large, for example 1000, we should creat a matrix of 1000*1000, it will lead to Memory Limit Exceeded.

After readed some article, I find this question is so tricky. Based on the solution of question 1 and question 2, we know how to calculate the max income at day i, and I we can reverse the process to calculate the max income from day i to the last day. Use v1 to save the max value from day 0 to day i, and v2 to save the max value from day i+1 to last day. And use an O(n) loop to find the final result. This solution use O(n) space and O(n) time.

Interesting! Although it looks like a very hard problem, the solustion is not difficult to understand.

##Best Time to Buy and Sell Stock IV
Question:

Say you have an array for which the ith element is the price of a given stock on day i.

Design an algorithm to find the maximum profit. You may complete at most k transactions.

Note:
You may not engage in multiple transactions at the same time (ie, you must sell the stock before you buy again).

Example:

Input: [2,4,1], k = 2

Output: 2

Explanation: Buy on day 1 (price = 2) and sell on day 2 (price = 4), profit = 4-2 = 2.

This problem expands two transactions in question 3 to k transactions. We need two variable, one is globally optimal solution, another is locally optimal solution. The globally optimal solution (global[i][j]) will save the maximum profit at day i with at most j transactions. The locally optimal solution (local[i][j]) will save the maximum profit at day i with at most j transaction howver, locally optimal solution will sell stock at day i. About the dynamic programming equation, global[i][j] = max(local[i][j],global[i-1][j]). Now, we should think about the local[i][j]. I think it is the hardest part in this question. The potential value of local are global[i-1][j-1]+max(price[i]-price[i-1],0) when price[i]>price[i-1] and local[i-1][j]+(price[i]-price[i-1]). global[i-1][j-1]+max(price[i]-price[i-1],0) represents when today’s price is higher than yesterday’s, we can cosider sell our stock. Why use global[i-1][j-1] not local[i-1][j-1]? Just as I said, local[i][j]) will save the maximum profit at day i with at most j transaction when last sell occur on day i. If I use local[i-1][j-1], I have sold my stock yesterday, so I can’t sold it today. Therefore, global[i-1][j-1]+max(price[i]-price[i-1],0) show the profit that I have finished at most j-1 transaction before day i-1, and I want sell stock at day i. Then, how about local[i-1][j]+(price[i]-price[i-1])? local[i-1][j]+(price[i]-price[i-1]) is the profit that I have finished j transactions at day i-1, and I try to postpone the last transaction from day i-1 to day i.

However, this solution have same problem. I get an error ‘terminate called after throwing an instance of ‘std::bad_alloc’ what(): std::bad_alloc.’ According to StackOverflow, it is caused by prices.size(). Then I changed the matrix size, add this k = min(k*2,(int)prices.size()); line to my solution, but it still throw an error, Memory Limit Exceeded. Yeah, when k = 1000 and pirces.size() is very large, the matrix will be more larger. So we need another way to presernt the matrix.
We can find that in the loop, we will use i and i-1, j and j-1, so we can use two 1-D vector.

This solution is an AC solution, I have a query, why I should iterate global from back to front.
